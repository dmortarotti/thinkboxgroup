WHO WE ARE: A collaborative cross-disciplinary team launched by ThinkBox Group and supported by The George Washington University
HOST: District of Columbia Housing Authority (DCHA), Washington, D.C.
WHAT WE DO: The Sustainable Communities Project® (SCP) is an innovative public-private philanthropic effort created to positively impact lives while transforming communities. In 2012, the SCP began work with seed funding from a District of Columbia sustainability and innovation grant to integrate communities and infrastructure. The SCP also manages the human capital component of DCHA’s Solar for All grant in support of its Sustainable Infrastructure Program. The SCP aims to address demonstrated needs and barriers to self-sufficiency plaguing underserved populations through a Four-Point Program: I. Jobs and Training II. Entrepreneurship III. Wellness IV. STEM Education
IMPACT TO DATE: ■ On track to generate 300 total jobs including 34 solar jobs to date
■ Trained 32 participants in solar and 1 lead-trainer for the National Science Foundation’s I-Corps™
■ Coaching 4 founders / potential founders
■ Hosted 2 pop-up entrepreneurship workshops and 1 green jobs training
■ Distributed over $5,000 in school-supplies to local communities
■ Funded over $1,000 in community grants
■ Provided 3 paid internships
■ Awarded 3 academic scholarships and 1 stipend totaling $10,500
■ Contributed over 3,500 volunteer hours to local communities
■ And more…
OUR PROCESS: The SCP utilizes a collaborative approach through Task Teams which address one or more tenant of the SCP’s Four-Point Programmatic Thrust. Task teams birth innovative, data-driven, strengths-based solutions to address ever-evolving challenges.
HOW WE DO IT: The Sustainable Communities Project® utilizes donations, sponsorships and volunteers from our partners, supporters and the community-at-large. The Think Better Fund, a component fund of the Greater Washington Community Foundation, provides seed funding for innovation, scholarships, internships, training for innovation among task teams and community-based organizations.
