Phyllis Wheatley YWCA

Reduced Yearly Operating Costs

$6,000

Funding Secured

$17M

Annual Emissions Reduced

114 MT of CO2

First constructed in 1920 as a safe gathering place for migrant African-American women, the four story facility currently provides 84 affordable rental units to the area’s low-income and vulnerable women.

The Phyllis Wheatley Young Women’s Christian Association (YWCA) redevelopment project ensures the long-term preservation of 84 units of affordable housing in a gentrifying neighborhood of Washington, D.C. PACE financed solar and energy efficiency upgrades take the renovated facility beyond basic building code, reducing operating costs for the facility by nearly $6,000 per year. This project marks the first use of PACE financing for a U.S. Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) assisted mixed-finance public housing property.

The annual value of electric and water utility savings realized by the project, lowering energy use by 24% and water use 47%, is estimated to exceed $73,000. Nearly $6,000 of the annual savings will accrue to the property owner, with the remainder for debt service on the project. The project will also avoid annual emissions of 114 metric tons of CO2 . More than $700,000 of funding provided by Amalgamated Bank and secured through the DC PACE program will allow the addition of a 30 kW solar PV array and other energy and water conservation equipment. PACE allows these permanent improvements to be added off balance sheet.

Over many engagements with offices within HUD, the DC Housing Authority, the Department of Human Services, and The Department of Housing and Community Development, Urban Ingenuity helped develop intercreditor agreements that ensure the property remains affordable housing for at least 40 years while maintaining compatibility with PACE security structures. Noting the novelty of this Low Income Housing Tax Credit project, the Director of the District’s Department of Energy and Environment said “This project is a model for the nation.”

“Paul and Daron brought their knowledge and skills to support Dante’s Partners in providing affordable housing for over 80 underserved women in Washington DC. In total we have realized energy and operations savings above projections to date and are on track to generate over $650K during the next several years.
