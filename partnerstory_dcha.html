In 2000, DCHA selected TBG as its energy partner to monitor and manage a $24M annual utility budget. In 2007 DCHA self-implemented a $24M energy capital improvement program with TBG as owner’s rep and together will implement a phase-2 $104M program starting in 2017. The phase 2 includes the development of 1.4 MW of on-site solar generation; as well as, a 1.6MW combined heating and cooling micro-turbine power system to be installed in the historic Langston Power Plant (1930) which is also being re-developed as a community based asset to support STEAM education for DCHA residents.





“ThinkBox Group has been supporting the sustainability goals of the District of Columbia Housing Authority since 1997. ThinkBox’s master plan has allowed us to be a pioneer in urban sustainability. That plan has helped us save of $50M in utility expenditures and to work to strengthen local communities through the catalyst of sustainability projects. Think Box is a strong, outcome-driven partner that will work with you to develop and implement groundbreaking projects that reduce cost, build underserved communities, and positively impact the environment."

Adrianne Todman
DCHA, Executive Director
