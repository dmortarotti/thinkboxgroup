Zion Baptist Church

Project Value

$1.6M

PACE Funding

$1.2M

Property Value

Increased by 20%

The $1.6M ZBC sustainable infrastructure project enhances the energy efficiency, water efficiency, reliability and thermal comfort performance of the sixty-year old Zion Baptist Church while dramatically reducing maintenance requirements.

Project scope includes the replacement of: a broken chiller system, an oversized hot water boiler and domestic hot water system; complete LED relighting, new windows and antiquated stormwater and sewage pumps.

Their new system includes a fully integrated, user-friendly operations technology platform which will allow maintenance staff to control and monitor the performance of the energy systems remotely. A 50 kW solar system provides free power to the Church while also recharging the sump pump 72-hour battery back-up system which will prevent boiler room flooding during power outages.

Greenworks Lending through the DC-PACE program provided $1.2M in construction funding. ThinkBox developed the project so that the annual savings covered the annual PACE assessment, allowing Zion Baptist Church to maximize the available capital from savings. As a result of the program, the Zion Baptist Church appraised value of their property increased by over 20%.
